💨 Air Pollutants & Their Impact

PM2.5

Particulate Matter

< 2.5 µm

Enters lungs, causes breathing issues
PM10

Particulate Matter

< 10 µm

Causes irritation and asthma
NO₂

Nitrogen Dioxide

Affects lungs, causes inflammation
SO₂

Sulfur Dioxide

Causes respiratory problems
CO

Carbon Monoxide

Reduces oxygen supply in blood
O₃

Ozone

ground-level

Causes chest pain & coughing

🧮 How is AQI Calculated?

1

Measure Pollutants

Concentration of each Pollutant is measured

↓
2

Convert to Sub-Index

Each pollutant concentration is converted into a sub-index value

↓
3

Find Maximum Value

The highest sub-index becomes the final AQI

📊

Formula:

AQI = Maximum (Sub-index of PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, SO₂, CO, O₃)

💡 Quick Example

If PM2.5 sub-index = 236, PM10 = 182, NO₂ = 78

Final AQI = 236 (Poor)

We consider the highest sub-index pollutant value as the AQI
