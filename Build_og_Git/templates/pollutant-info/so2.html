SO₂

Sulfur Dioxide

🔍 What is SO₂?

Sulfur Dioxide (SO₂) is a colorless gas with a pungent, suffocating odor. It's produced when sulfur-containing fuels are burned.

SO₂ is a major contributor to acid rain and can cause serious respiratory problems.

📍 Sources of SO₂

Coal Power Plants
The largest single source of SO₂ emissions globally.
Industrial Facilities
Smelters, refineries, and cement plants release SO₂ during operations.
Diesel Engines
Ships, trucks, trains, and heavy machinery burning sulfur-rich fuel.
Petroleum Refining
Oil and gas processing releases sulfur compounds during refining.
Metal Processing
Copper, zinc, and iron smelting produce SO₂ as a byproduct.
Natural Sources
Volcanic eruptions and hot springs release SO₂ naturally.

⚠️ Health Effects

Respiratory System
  • Airway constriction
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Throat irritation
  • Worsening of asthma
At-Risk Groups
  • People with asthma
  • Children
  • Elderly individuals
  • Those with heart disease

🌍 Environmental Impact

  • Acid Rain — Major cause of acid rain damaging forests and lakes
  • Vegetation Damage — Harms crops, trees, and plants
  • Water Acidification — Damages aquatic ecosystems
  • Building Corrosion — Deteriorates stone and metal structures
  • Visibility — Forms fine particles reducing visibility

🛡️ Protection Measures

  • Stay Indoors — When SO₂ levels are high
  • Use Clean Energy — Support transition from coal to renewables
  • Avoid Industrial Areas — Stay away from factories and refineries
  • Monitor AQI — Check sulfur dioxide levels before outdoor activities
  • Respiratory Protection — Use appropriate masks in high-risk areas
