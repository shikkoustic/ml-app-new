PM10

Particulate Matter < 10 µm

🔍 What is PM10?

PM10 refers to coarse particulate matter with a diameter of 10 micrometers or smaller. While larger than PM2.5, these particles can still be inhaled and cause health problems.

PM10 particles are about 7 times thinner than a human hair.

📍 Sources of PM10

Road Dust
Traffic, tire wear, and brake wear lift coarse particles from road surfaces.
Construction
Demolition and crushing operations release large quantities of coarse dust.
Agriculture
Plowing, harvesting, and livestock activity stir soil particles into the air.
Mining
Quarrying and mineral processing generate significant coarse particulate loads.
Unpaved Roads
Vehicles on dirt roads continuously disturb loose surface dust.
Natural Sources
Sea salt spray, pollen, and soil erosion contribute naturally to PM10 levels.

⚠️ Health Effects

Respiratory Issues
  • Irritation of airways
  • Worsening of asthma symptoms
  • Reduced lung function
  • Increased respiratory infections
Vulnerable Groups
  • Children
  • Elderly people
  • People with asthma or COPD
  • Outdoor workers

🛡️ Protection Measures

  • Face Masks — N95 masks filter out PM10 particles
  • Limit Outdoor Exercise — Exercise indoors on high pollution days
  • Close Windows — Keep dust out during windy conditions
  • Wet Cleaning — Use damp cloth to clean surfaces
  • Avoid Dusty Areas — Stay away from construction sites
  • Vehicle Maintenance — Keep car windows closed in traffic
